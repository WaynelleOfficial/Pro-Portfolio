BuizGoals AI

Description:

During a 48-hour hackathon hosted by the UCI AI Club in partnership with AWS, I was challenged to develop an application that either enhanced productivity through cloud infrastructure or create a searchable knowledge base or create a data explorer for personal/academic use.

Given the time constraints, I chose to tackle a challenge faced by small business owners: staying productive and making meaningful progress toward their goals. The result was BuizGoals AI, an AI-powered tool designed to help entrepreneurs set and achieve actionable objectives.

BuizGoals AI enhances productivity by generating S.M.A.R.T. goals (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound), tailored to each user's business aspirations. The application leverages cloud-based AI services, AWS Bedrock, to guide users with clear, achievable steps, thus making long-term progress more manageable and time-effective for small business owners.

Tech:

GitHub, React, Node, Html, CSS, JavaScript, Python, ASW Bedrock, FastAPI, Next.js


App Demo:

This video showcases BuizGoals AI.

The app receives a user prompt through a responsive frontend built with React, Next.js, HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. I used Next.js for efficient routing and server-side rendering, ensuring fast load times and a dynamic user experience. When a prompt is submitted, it's routed to the backend, where I implemented an AI service layer using Python and FastAPI. This service securely communicates with AWS Bedrock, leveraging the capabilities of Anthropic’s Claude v2 to generate context-aware, S.M.A.R.T. goals tailored to the user's input. The generated response is then sent back through the API and seamlessly rendered on the frontend using React components. All code is version-controlled and collaboratively managed on GitHub, enabling streamlined development, CI/CD, and deployment integration.

This project demonstrates a full-stack, cloud-integrated architecture combining modern frameworks and AI services to deliver a practical, productivity-focused solution.

    Amazon Gamefied Call Center Capstone

    Description:

    Tasked to creata a proof of concept of a gameified dashboard for call centers for Amazon, my group of 1 computer science major, 1 software enginneeing major, and 3 informatics major got to work over a 20 week period. The primary users were call agents and their supervisors. We created an app that works to enhance agents' performance and motivation through the implementation of gamification techniques in call centers utilizing Amazon Connect, fostering a spirit of healthy competition and collaboration within the work environment.

    Tech:

    Figma, GitHub, Amazon Connect, React, Node, JavaScript/JavaScript XML (JS/JSX), Bootstrap/Material Design


    App Demo:

    The leaderboard is intended to be displayed on a large central screen, showing the top five agents for each metric. This discourages toxicity toward those below the top 5 as well as informing them who may be able to help them improve if they are struggling.

    The supervisor dashboard provides full visibility into team members' performance data, enabling effective monitoring. Supervisors can create challenges to foster teamwork, award trophies to agents, and access a full team leaderboard for a holistic view of all members, not just the top 5.

    Agents are able to view only their own ranks, streaks, and trophies. They are also able to view team-assigned challenges that the supervisor created. They can also navigate to the limited leaderboards that only show the top 5 agents, for ease of viewing.

    Impact:

      Supervisor Dashboard: Designed and implemented the Figma prototype, focusing on both visual design and functional elements.
      Innovative Features: Proposed the concept of “challenges,” advocating for its inclusion in the final product to enhance user engagement.

    • Challenges and Lessons Learned:

    • Adapting Methodologies for Efficiency:
      At the start of the project, our team relied on a waterfall approach, completing low-fidelity designs, high-fidelity designs, and development sequentially for the entire project. This process was slow, with significant delays caused by waiting for approval to move to the next phase, which left the team feeling stagnant.
      To address this, we re-evaluated our workflow and adopted agile methodologies during sprint planning. This shift allowed for iterative progress, more dynamic collaboration, and greater adaptability to feedback, significantly improving team morale and productivity.
      Addressing Ethical Implications:
      One key challenge was considering the ethical implications of how our application might pressure call agents in the workplace. To mitigate this, we developed a comprehensive ethics document with strategies to prioritize fairness and well-being, including:
    • Leaderboard Design: Displaying only the top 5 agents on the public central leaderboard to avoid exposing lower-ranking agents, preserving their dignity and morale.
      Leaderboard Resets: Resetting leaderboard rankings every 90 days to give all employees a fresh opportunity to excel and reduce prolonged pressure on top-ranking agents.
      Break-Time Metrics Exclusion: Excluding assigned break times from performance metrics to ensure employees feel comfortable taking their breaks without the fear of being penalized or tempted to overwork.
      • These measures helped create a balanced system that encouraged healthy competition while respecting individual well-being, ensuring that our product could drive motivation without compromising ethics.

    Next Steps:

    Understanding that this is a proof of concept, there are key areas that need further development to refine this gamified call center. We recommend to develop a user interface and functionality to set clear challenge goals that take into account real data obtained from each agent, updating trophies to agent dashboard upon completion of challenges, and adding trophies to an agent dashboards, when a supervisor assigns individual trophies.
